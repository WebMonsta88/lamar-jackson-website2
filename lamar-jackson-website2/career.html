Entering the NFL

Lamar Jackson was drafted by the Baltimore Ravens in the first round of the 2018 NFL Draft. Many questioned whether Jackson's unconventional style of play would succeed in the NFL, but Lamar silenced his critics in spectacular fashion. His incredible dual-threat ability became a game-changer for the Ravens.

Jackson’s rookie season was impressive, but it was in his second year that he truly broke out. He led the Ravens to an outstanding 14-2 record and won the NFL MVP award. Lamar became the youngest player in NFL history to win the MVP award, and his unique combination of arm strength and speed kept defenses guessing every game.

Key Career Achievements

  • 2019 NFL MVP
  • First quarterback in NFL history to lead the league in passing touchdowns (2019)
  • Multiple Pro Bowl selections
  • Record-breaking rushing yards for a quarterback

More About Lamar Jackson

Lamar Jackson's legacy is continuing to grow. While the Ravens’ offense has evolved with Jackson at the helm, his style of play is already influencing the next generation of quarterbacks. His speed, vision, and leadership have made him a true icon in the NFL.

Beyond the stats, Jackson is known for his humility, work ethic, and commitment to his community. He continues to inspire athletes worldwide and remains a positive role model in the sport of football.
